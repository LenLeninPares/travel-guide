The best time to visit the Philippines is during the dry season, from **November to April**, when you can enjoy:

- **Clear skies and sunny days** — Perfect for beach activities, hiking, and outdoor exploration
- **Comfortable temperatures** — Around 25-30°C (77-86°F), ideal for sightseeing
- **Lower humidity** — Much more pleasant compared to the rainy season
- **Fewer typhoons** — The typhoon season runs from June to October, so this period is safest
- **Water activities** — Calm seas make diving, snorkeling, and island hopping excellent

**Peak Season (December-January):** This is the busiest time with higher prices and crowded attractions. Book accommodations in advance!

**Shoulder Season (February-April):** Great weather with fewer crowds than December-January. March-April can get hot but remains dry and sunny.

🍽️ Food Tips for Visitors

Eat where the locals eat — Carinderias (local eateries) offer authentic and affordable meals.
Try the rice combos — Many dishes are best enjoyed with steamed white rice; don't skip it!
Ask about spice levels — Some dishes can be unexpectedly spicy; always ask before ordering.
Visit wet markets in the morning — The freshest produce, seafood, and local ingredients are available early.
Merienda is a thing — Filipinos enjoy a mid-morning and mid-afternoon snack break; join in!
Bring cash — Many street vendors and small eateries do not accept cards or digital payments.
Watch for seasonal specialties — Certain snacks like bibingka and puto bumbong are only available during the Christmas season.

🧡 Food Culture Notes

Food in the Philippines is more than sustenance — it's a social activity. Meals are often shared family-style (boodle fight style), and it's common to be offered food as a sign of hospitality. Always say "Kain tayo!" (Let's eat!) to make a great impression!
